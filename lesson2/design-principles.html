Visual Hierarchy

Organization used: USA Today

When talking about visual heriarchy, sizes is what most people think of. For hundred of years magazines have printed things with different sizes in order to indicate importance and significance. A newspapaer is an example of this, often having a big picture and a headline to catch interest. This picture is often about the most recent important event. Then medium pictures are presented, for less important news. By doing so, a heriarchy is created where the pictures and their position on the page represent importance.

Rule of Thirds

Organization used: National Geographic

The National Geographic page has a bunch of pictures that represent the rule of thirds. Not only that, but it also has three defined components, leaving the middle third of the page for the consumer to see and focus on. As the rule of third states, this creates a sense of flow and direction for the pages.
